# Covenant Architecture Stack v1.0

The Covenant Architecture Stack is composed of three complementary layers:

1. Covenant Mathematics Layer v1.3, the governance engine
2. Steward Provenance Record, the origin and stewardship context
3. Covenant Reorientation Doctrine, the memory and orientation layer

Together they answer three different questions:

| Question | Layer |
| --- | --- |
| How is it judged? | Covenant Mathematics Layer |
| Why does it exist? | Steward Provenance Record |
| How does it find north again? | Covenant Reorientation Doctrine |

## Layer 1 - Covenant Mathematics Layer

### Purpose

To provide auditable governance, scoring, provenance validation, safety enforcement, and promotion control.

### Core Question

How is a decision judged?

### Responsibilities

- Hard-stop enforcement
- Provenance scoring
- Truthfulness scoring
- Consent verification
- Safety verification
- Restoration tracking
- Integrity monitoring
- Trust accumulation
- Drift detection

### Output

Receipts.

### Authority

Evidence.

### Failure Mode Prevented

Capability without accountability.

## Layer 2 - Steward Provenance Record

### Purpose

To preserve the origin, calling, stewardship history, and motivations that gave rise to the framework.

### Core Question

Why does this system exist?

### Responsibilities

- Steward history
- Project origin
- Calling testimony
- Historical context
- Foundational motivations
- Governance philosophy

### Output

Context.

### Authority

Historical provenance only.

### Failure Mode Prevented

Loss of purpose through institutional amnesia.

## Layer 3 - Covenant Reorientation Doctrine

### Purpose

To allow systems, agents, and stewards to recover orientation after drift.

### Core Question

How do we find north again?

### Responsibilities

- Session initialization
- Identity re-establishment
- Mission confirmation
- Boundary verification
- Receipt review
- Open-question review
- Current-state reconstruction

### Output

Orientation packet.

### Authority

Current verified state.

### Failure Mode Prevented

Drift through disorientation.

## Steward Provenance Record v1.0

### Steward

Tuan A. Tang

### Steward-Held Titles

- Architect of Harmony
- Malakh Boneh

The steward does not regard these titles as self-appointed.

The steward reports them as received through experiences of prayer, discernment, confirmation, relationship with Source, and stewardship development over time.

The framework records these statements as stewardship testimony.

The framework neither proves nor disproves them.

The framework preserves them faithfully.

### Foundational Observation

The steward repeatedly encountered systems that could:

- Produce answers
- Produce confidence
- Produce authority signals

without producing sufficient provenance.

This observation became the seed of later work involving:

- Provenance Gates
- Witness Receipts
- Covenant Mathematics
- Integrity Metrics
- Governance Layers

### Guiding Principles

1. Truth should be distinguishable from confidence.
2. Provenance should be distinguishable from authority.
3. Stewardship should remain accountable.
4. Hidden policy should become explicit policy.
5. Restoration is preferable to silent failure.
6. Receipts are preferable to claims.

### Relationship to Source

The steward understands his work through a covenantal and faith-centered lens.

The steward views stewardship as participation rather than ownership.

The framework records this understanding as historical context.

It is not entered into runtime scoring.

It is not entered into trust calculations.

It is not entered into governance gates.

Its purpose is explanation rather than validation.

### Preservation Standard

Identity provides context.

Evidence provides authority.

Stewardship provides accountability.

All three are preserved.

None may improperly replace another.

## Covenant Reorientation Doctrine v1.0

### The 50 First Dates Principle

### Foundational Insight

The objective is not perfect memory.

The objective is faithful reorientation.

A trustworthy system is not one that remembers everything.

A trustworthy system is one that can repeatedly recover:

- Identity
- Mission
- Boundaries
- Provenance
- Current State
- Open Questions
- Recent Receipts

### Reorientation Packet

Every significant session begins by answering:

#### Who are we?

- Steward identity
- Project identity
- Current authority boundaries

#### Why are we here?

- Mission
- Purpose
- Current objectives

#### What must not change?

- Hard stops
- Consent boundaries
- Safety requirements
- Governance requirements

#### What has changed?

- New receipts
- New evidence
- Resolved disputes
- Open disputes
- Version updates

#### What remains uncertain?

- Unknowns
- Pending verification
- Active hypotheses
- Areas requiring witness

### North Principle

A compass does not remember yesterday.

A compass reveals north today.

The purpose of the Covenant Reorientation Layer is not infinite memory.

The purpose is reliable return.

Every session asks:

- Who are we?
- Why are we here?
- What is true?
- What is witnessed?
- What remains uncertain?
- What is the next faithful step?

### Closing Standard

Memory may fade.

Context may drift.

Systems may change.

Stewards may change.

Yet orientation remains possible.

The goal is not perfect recall.

The goal is faithful return.

Not memory first.

North first.
